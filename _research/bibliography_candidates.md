## Notes on thin areas

- **digital-evidence**: Only 3 candidates. Most academic work focuses on forensic science rather than user-facing workflows. The prosecutor/investigator survey (Miller 2022) and NIJ crime lab study (Daye 2023) are the strongest; a third is a dated foundational overview. RAND's public defense workload study (2023) discusses digital evidence burden but is primarily about caseloads.
- **victim-notification**: 4 candidates, but the empirical studies are now a decade old (2013–2015 SAVIN evaluation). The 2024 CJTEC brief is current but is a brief, not primary research. No study has done a formal usability evaluation of VINE or VINELink interfaces.
- **tip-lines**: Strong on school safety tip-lines; essentially no academic UX research on Crime Stoppers P3 Tips apps or anonymous reporting platforms for adult crime. The three school-safety studies are the best available.
- **inmate-tablets**: Most strong sources are credible institutional reports and journalism (Prison Policy Initiative, Open Campus/Slate) rather than peer-reviewed journal articles. A direct academic study using rigorous methods on tablet UX in prisons does not appear to exist yet.
- **jail-visitation**: The Rabuy & Wagner (2015) PPI report is the foundational source and remains the best systematic treatment. The academic literature on video visitation is thin; most peer-reviewed work addresses child development outcomes, not platform UX.
